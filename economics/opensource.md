# Why must Iguana X become open source

The rise of **Git** in software development is a powerful lesson about the indispensable value of open source for critical technological infrastructure. In the early 2000s, Linux kernel development relied on BitKeeper, a closed-source version control system that, though gifted to the community for free, could be withdrawn at any time. This dependency created an underlying tension; the open source community was always at the mercy of a single vendor’s decisions. When the free licenses were eventually revoked, the Linux team found themselves suddenly vulnerable—losing the essential tool that powered their global project.

This crisis sparked swift innovation: Linus Torvalds created Git, an open source, distributed, and robust alternative that delivered speed, integrity, and freedom from corporate control. Git’s rapid adoption and success came not only from its technical excellence but also from the reassurance it gave users—no single entity could ever again hold the infrastructure hostage. As a result, Git became a foundation of modern software development, powering millions of projects and platforms worldwide.

The lesson is clear: when critical infrastructure is proprietary, users risk vendor lock-in, lack of transparency, and stifled innovation. Open source, by contrast, offers resilience, fosters trust through transparency, and accelerates progress through community collaboration. The trajectory of Git proves that, over time, open solutions naturally win in foundational technologies because they guarantee independence, adaptability, and a future owned by all users—not just one company.
