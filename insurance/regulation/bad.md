# Why are medical devices so bad?

Most people assume, wrongly that medical devices are well designed
and put together by highly skilled engineers.

Sadly no.  The opposite is true.

I enjoy helping my client Natus but they were a really hard account
to grow.  The problem is medical device engineers are petrified at
the idea of making any significant improvements to their technology.

A small improvement results in a pile of compliance paperwork.

Just not that fun ;-)

It's not all bad from a business point of view - high regulatory 
hurdles make the technology really expensive - so Natus and other
medical device technology companies sell smaller volumes of very
high margin technology which is a long way from where it could
be compared to a team executing via the [theory of constrainst](/system/toc).

But the result is that sadly western medical technology is expensive
and mediocre.  

The 'bad guys' have better tech.  But that's just a label.
