# Avoiding Loss-Leading Consulting

One of the key lessons I learned from running my previous company is the risk of undervaluing specialized expertise. Over time, I noticed a shift: much of our team’s energy was devoted to what I would now call loss-leading consulting. Despite delivering an industry-leading product—renowned for its speed, stability, and ease of use—we often found ourselves undercharging enterprise clients relative to the significant value we provided.

Unfortunately, this dynamic led to a situation where consulting became less of a value-add and more of a drain on our resources, particularly when working with prospects who never transitioned to full clients. At one point, we even partnered with an external consulting firm whose rates exceeded what we charged our own clients, ultimately resulting in unsustainable margins.

Recognizing these challenges was pivotal for me. As a sole proprieter today, I have much greater flexibility and control. With limited hours each day and strong demand, I can focus on partnerships that are mutually beneficial. If a prospective client requires more attention than I can commit to, I’m happy to refer them to a trusted affiliate partner. And if a project’s budget does not reflect the level of expertise, time, and value required, I am transparent in explaining that premium results naturally entail a premium investment.

It’s all about ensuring alignment—delivering great value while maintaining a healthy, sustainable business relationship for everyone involved.

Basically if you are expecting me to finance your business the answer is no.

If your hospital clients impose layers of expense through bureucracy, poorly thought out compliance procedures, then all these costs will be charged for or the business ignored entirely.

My time is valuable and it's not easy finding competent affiliates who want to deal with nonsense either.
