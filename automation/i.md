# iNTERFACEWARE Transition

iNTERFACEWARE is transitioning to a new much leaner more efficient model which will result in much better client outcomes as energy is needed is focused where it needs to be.

It was pretty crazy, given that we were essentially a one-product company — one legacy generation and one new generation — yet roughly 20 of our 27 staff were occupied with custom contracts, administration, and other things.

I’m going to stop responding to and following up on most individual emails. Instead, I’m switching into a mode of solving the major problems while I iteratively build the new infrastructure.

That will include self-service tools for license generation, contracts, W-8 forms, ESG questionnaires, training, accounting, CRM, and an affiliate network for customers who need a higher level of service than one sole proprietor in the Cayman Islands can provide.

I solved an immediate problem is restoring access to the legacy products.  That took about half an hour with the right question.  See https://github.com/eliotmuirgrid/downloads

The most logical thing for customers is to follow me on LinkedIn. As I solve each major problem, I’ll post about it there. I expect to resolve most of the important issues quite quickly.

But I’ll be leaving it to customers to follow up if their particular needs still aren’t being met. Trying to manually manage hundreds of customer conversations through email is simply too fiddly until I build some automation — and right now there is much lower-hanging fruit that will help far more people.
