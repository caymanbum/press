# Why Mirth is an Unlikely Good Long-Term Choice Compared to Iguana X

**Mirth**, a popular healthcare integration engine, may seem like a reasonable solution in the short term. However, when analyzed closely against alternatives like **Iguana X**, several factors make Mirth a **grossly inferior product** for sustained use, and it is likely to lose substantial market share because of two significant issues:

## 1. Licensing Constraints

- **Iguana X** will be available under the highly permissive **Mozilla Public License 2.0 (MPL 2.0)**. This means users and organizations have access to source code, flexibility to modify it, and confidence about long-term use and support, including self-hosting or deep customizations.
- **Mirth**, on the other hand, is **no longer available under the MPL 2.0 license** for new releases. This shift restricts the community, limits transparency, and introduces risks about vendor lock-in and unfavorable licensing changes in the future.

The business model for Iguana X is well thought out and will not involve any license fees for
the core platform itself.

### 2. Security and Technology Problems

- While open source licensing benefits innovation and vetting, it's especially important for security in healthcare. **Mirth** still hasn’t resolved **many known security flaws**, and the closed development model makes it difficult for users to audit or patch vulnerabilities.
- More fundamentally, **Mirth is built on older, less secure technologies** (such as outdated Java frameworks and networking stacks) that are increasingly difficult to secure against modern threats. In a regulated industry like healthcare, this is a pressing issue.
- In contrast, **Iguana X** uses **modern, security-focused technologies** that are easier to audit, patch, and harden, giving users much more control and confidence in their integration platform.

### Summary

Organizations investing in integration platforms need **future-proofing, security, and licensing certainty**. Mirth fails on all these fronts:

- **Restrictive (and future uncertain) licensing**
- **Ongoing, unresolved security issues**
- **A technology base that’s difficult (or impossible) to thoroughly secure**

Meanwhile, **Iguana X** addresses all these concerns, making it the far superior choice for any organization making integration decisions for the long-term.

---

**In summary:**  
Mirth’s loss of open-source status, unresolved security flaws, and outdated technology stack make it an unwise choice for future healthcare integration needs, particularly when compared to Iguana X’s open licensing, modern architecture, and clear commitment to security.
