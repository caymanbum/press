# Reality Check for iNTERFACEWARE Customers

Many iNTERFACEWARE customers—especially those in larger, more bureaucratic organizations—were often unaware of how the company actually operated. Contrary to appearances, the company’s success was not the result of a team effort, but rather the extraordinary work of a single gifted engineer, Eliot, who found himself surrounded by a staff that was, frankly, much less skilled.

Over time, these less capable staff members grew in number and eventually overshadowed Eliot, making it increasingly difficult for customers to see where the true value of the company lay. Many became confused and believed they were benefiting from the various [Service Level Agreements (SLAs)](sla.md) and [Statements of Work](statement.md) provided to them, not realizing that much of the value came directly from Eliot’s expertise.

As a result, Eliot grew frustrated and felt increasingly isolated from his own customers—unable to communicate clearly or control the processes as he once did.

Now, it’s time to clear up the confusion and explain how our payment processes and other core policies must operate moving forward. Below, you’ll find direct links and explanations for key materials every iNTERFACEWARE customer should review:

- [Bureaucracy](bureaucracy.md): Understanding how internal and external bureaucracies affect service delivery and communication.
- [Busy](busy.md): The impact of unnecessary activities and distractions on productivity and customer value.
- [ESG](esg.md): Our approach to Environmental, Social, and Governance standards.
- [Free](free.md): Good products like Iguana are not “free”.
- [Mail](mail.md): Mailing address for Eliot if you are unable to use email.
- [Payment](payment.md): How to pay Eliot.
- [Pricing](pricing.md): Explaining how pricing has to work.
- [Security](security.md): Considerations of the risks of using inferior alternatives.
- [SLA](sla.md): What to expect from Eliot's Service Level Agreements.
- [Statement](statement.md): Clarity around the absense of Statements of Work and project deliverables.
- [Tax](tax.md): Understanding tax rules for paying Eliot.
- [W8BEN](w8ben.md): Get yourW-8BEN forms here - needed for some American customers.

By reviewing these documents, customers will gain clarity on how we now operate, what value you can expect, and how to ensure a productive and mutually beneficial relationship moving forward.
