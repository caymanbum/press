# Viral Genome Types

Viruses can have:
- **DNA genomes** (either double-stranded or single-stranded [DNA](dna.md))
- **RNA genomes** (either double-stranded or single-stranded [RNA](rna.md))

## Distribution
- In terms of **known viral species**, **RNA viruses** make up a **very large proportion** of described viruses, especially those infecting animals and plants.
- However, when you include *all* viruses (including bacteriophages that infect bacteria), **DNA viruses** may actually outnumber RNA viruses overall, particularly in the environment.

## Key Points:
- **Among viruses that infect humans:** Many high-profile viruses are RNA viruses (e.g., influenza, HIV, SARS-CoV-2, hepatitis C, Ebola).
- **In the total viral world:** DNA viruses, especially bacteriophages, are extremely abundant.
- **In diversity:** RNA viruses are very diverse because of their fast mutation rates and adaptability.

## Estimates (as of 2024 knowledge):
- It's not accurate to say “most” viruses are RNA-based globally; but among human and plant pathogens, RNA viruses are highly significant.
- Among all viruses by **biomass and abundance** (e.g., ocean phages), DNA viruses dominate.

## In summary:
**A large proportion, but not the majority, of all viruses are RNA-based. It depends on the context and which hosts you consider.**

In viruses affecting humans, you'll see a disproportionate number of RNA viruses. For viruses generally, most environmental viruses (especially bacteriophages) have DNA genomes.
