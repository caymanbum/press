# Paul Conyngham

**Paul Conyngham** is a Sydney-based Australian AI and technology entrepreneur. His personal story took a unique turn when his dog, Rosie—a spirited eight-year-old Staffordshire bull terrier–Shar Pei cross—was diagnosed with aggressive mast cell cancer after earlier misdiagnoses. Despite surgery, chemotherapy, and additional treatments, Rosie's cancer continued to progress.

---

## A Pioneering Approach to Treatment

What makes Rosie's story remarkable is not just the treatment itself, but the collaboration and innovation involved:

1. **Genomic Sequencing**
   - Conyngham funded sequencing of both Rosie's normal DNA and her tumor DNA.

2. **AI-Powered Analysis**
   - Using AI tools—including ChatGPT and AlphaFold—Conyngham analyzed the genomic data to identify **neoantigens**: mutated proteins unique to Rosie's cancer that could potentially be targeted by her immune system.

3. **Bringing in the Experts**
   - Instead of trying to develop a treatment on his own, Conyngham connected with experts at the University of New South Wales, specifically the Ramaciotti Centre for Genomics and the RNA Institute.
   - Researchers reviewed the data and designed a personalized, experimental mRNA vaccine encoding several of Rosie's tumor-specific mutations.

4. **Treatment and Supervision**
   - Under veterinary research oversight, Rosie received the vaccine, along with booster doses and other cancer care interventions.

---

## Reported Results

- **Tumor Response:** Most of Rosie's tumors reportedly shrank substantially—by approximately 75%, according to some reports.
- **Improved Wellbeing:** Rosie’s mobility and energy saw notable improvements.
- **Next Steps:** Because one resistant tumor remained, the team developed a second-generation, personalized vaccine targeting additional mutations.
