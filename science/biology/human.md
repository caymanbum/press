# Human DNA

- Human [DNA](dna.md) is the molecule that carries our genetic instructions.
- It is organized into 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total) in most cells.
- Each person inherits half their DNA from their mother and half from their father.
- The human genome consists of about 3 billion base pairs.
- About 99.9% of human DNA is identical from person to person; the remaining differences account for individual uniqueness.

Now, let’s **bring up the concept of pedigree collapse:**

When we look at human ancestry, it might seem at first like each generation back doubles the number of ancestors. For example: two parents, four grandparents, eight great-grandparents, and so on. However, this isn't sustainable over many generations—going back just 10 generations suggests over 1,000 ancestors, and a few hundred more would yield more ancestors than have ever existed!

This is where **pedigree collapse** comes in. Pedigree collapse occurs when ancestors occupy multiple slots in your family tree because distant relatives marry each other (even unknowingly). This means that, instead of having completely unique ancestors in every branch, some individuals appear multiple times. In reality, human populations have always been relatively small and interconnected, so many lines of descent "collapse" onto the same people.

Fact is most people have trouble tracing their geneology back more that 3 generations.
